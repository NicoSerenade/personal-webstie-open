About Me

01

Born in 2004 in Cali, Colombia, I came into the world as Nicolás Castañeda Martínez. I grew up in a school rooted in arts, embodied practices, and spirituality, where the question "How can we live better lives?" became central to my thinking. This early curiosity led me through diverse pursuits—from physics and music composition to earning a black belt in Karate and competing in several sports. These experiences cultivated a deep appreciation for discipline, well-being, and interdisciplinary thinking.

02

Between ages 15 and 18, I had the profound opportunity to travel and live within indigenous communities across Colombia—from the Ticuna in Amazonas to the Wayuu in Guajira and the Arhuaco in Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta. Living with them as they lived, I internalized essential worldviews that fundamentally expanded my understanding of humanity and continue to guide how I approach life and work today.

03

At 19, I discovered AI and taught myself to code. I was fascinated by its potential to solve human problems, as well as concerned about its equal capacity to worsen them. That tension sparked my commitment to building technology with integrity—not just for profit, but for genuine human flourishing.

Around the same time, I was deeply immersed in Buddhist practices and seriously contemplating life in a monastery. Then I earned a full academic scholarship to study Systems Engineering and recognized it as a pivotal moment—an opportunity to merge my humanistic insights with technical skills in pursuit of materializing positive change in the world.

04

I'm currently studying Systems Engineering with a minor in Anthropology at Universidad de Los Andes, Bogotá, while pursuing entrepreneurial ventures, composing music, and writing from time to time. Everything I do revolves around an evolved question: "How can we create technology that helps us live better lives?" This question drives everything—from my academic research and professional pursuits to my entrepreneurial ventures and personal life.

Beyond the Work

When I'm not coding or studying, you'll often find me practicing meditation, yoga, playing chess, or dancing to my own rhythm. I'm passionate about sports and outdoor adventures, composing music, and exploring the forces that shape how we live today—often writing reflections about these observations to better understand our evolving world.

Contemplative Practices

Through meditation and yoga I learn to cultivate inner stillness and clarity, allowing me to stay centered amid chaos and act with intention.

Creative Expression

Through music and writing I learn to think beyond conventional boundaries, express complex ideas with nuance, and transform abstract concepts into tangible forms.

Physical Engagement

Through active challenges I learn to think strategically under pressure, build resilience, and cultivate the discipline required for mastery.
